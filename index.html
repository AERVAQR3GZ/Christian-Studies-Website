A breif overview of Cosmological Argument


In the beginning God made this world,
That is a phrase that almost every body will recognise, but not all people will get behind.
This blog will discuss the Cosmological Argument and show how it has caused me to
believe that Jesus and God are not real, although the reasons to do so are valid and applicable.
It has 3 main points:

Everything has a cause.

Since we know the universe was created at a finite point in the past, it must have had a cause . Since there was nothing before the universe, the cause must be outside of the universe, and God is outside of this universe, force that is almighty and unworldly. a This strongly suggests that God created the universe.

The universe has a cause.

Since we know the universe is expanding, and thanks to the second law of thermo dynamics, we know that the universe is not eternal. This means that the universe must have a cause . Since there was nothing before the universe, the cause must have been divine, with purpose and intended, which matches perfectly with God making the universe.

God made the universe

Since the universe has to have a cause, and it can not have caused its self, that means that God created it.

This argument may result in:

This argument may result in a belief in God because it has irrefutable surface level arguments that are simple and easy to comprehend with little thought or understanding of the creation of the universe needed to agree with it.

Limitations

This argument has a few key limitations, in which I will cover 3 .
The main three are the paradox of no time, the self contradiction and the lack of evidence towards which specific thing caused it.

Infinite "Time"

Since before the universe was created, there was no measure of time , infinite time could have passed, and with infinite passing time leads to some guaranteed something , in fact guaranteed everything, hence the universe!

Self contradiction

With the argument that everything has a cause , it brings up the trouble that God must have had a cause . This is one of the clearest and simplest reasons that this argument is flawed.

Why God?

If the creation of the universe was by some other divine being (and not the big bang), what is saying it has to be God? There is no clear point leading us to believe that it must have been God. It could have been any other divine being .

How it has changed me.

Before studying this argument, I used to think that the reasons for belief in God were nonexistent, and simply used as self-comfort for what comes after death.

Now I think that there are several well-structured and well formatted arguments for and against the existence of God and the origin of the universe, yet I still strongly believe that God is not real.

Therefore, the argument has made we want to consider Christianity more seriously, as it has showed me that there are often believable reasons for the existence of God, the validity of Christianity and the existence of Jesus. I give this argument a 3/5, as it is easy to understand, yet has some critical flaws.
